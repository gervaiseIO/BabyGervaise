
# Gervaise Vision

Version: v0.3
Status: Revised Vision Draft
Date: 2026

---

## Introduction

Gervaise is an **AI‑native computing system** designed around:

- local‑first intelligence
- continuous human interaction
- activity‑first computing
- distributed execution across personal devices

Unlike modern AI tools, Gervaise does not revolve around chat threads, prompts, or agent sessions.

Instead, it is built around **one persistent intelligence** that interprets interaction and orchestrates work.

The constraints that protect this vision are defined in:

**GERVAISE_PRODUCT_LAWS.md**

---

## The Core Idea

Gervaise transforms human interaction into structured work.

The runtime pipeline is:

Human Input  
→ Human–Gervaise Interaction Engine (HGIE)  
→ Activity Graph  
→ Orchestrator  
→ Execution

The user interacts with one system.

The system internally manages structured activities.

---

## Continuous Intelligence

Gervaise exposes **one continuous interface**.

Users never manage:

- chat threads
- sessions
- assistant instances

They interact with one system that remembers and continues work.

This follows **Product Law #1 — One Continuous Interface**.

---

## Activity‑First Computing

All meaningful work becomes an **Activity**.

Examples:

- summarize documents
- continue a report
- message a contact
- analyze code

Activities may run in parallel, persist over time, and move across devices.

This reflects **Product Law #2 — Activities Are the System**.

---

## The System as the Environment

Gervaise acts as the central environment where work happens.

Instead of navigating between apps, the user interacts with Gervaise directly.

External services integrate through capabilities rather than separate interfaces.

This reflects **Product Law #4 — Gervaise Is the Environment**.

---

## Model‑Agnostic Intelligence

Gervaise does not expose model selection to the user.

The runtime manages internal model slots such as:

- fast
- deep

The orchestrator selects models automatically.

This follows **Product Law #6 — Models Are Invisible**.

---

## Local‑First Distributed Runtime

Every installation of Gervaise is a **node**.

Nodes may include:

- Gervaise Mac
- Gervaise Android
- Gervaise iOS
- future desktop systems
- optional cloud nodes

Nodes synchronize to form a trusted personal system.

This reflects **Product Law #8 — Local First**.

---

## Deterministic Control

While models assist interpretation, the system remains deterministic.

The orchestrator controls:

- scheduling
- execution
- node placement
- policy enforcement

This ensures reliability and explainability.

This follows **Product Law #9 — Deterministic Control**.

---

## Long‑Term Vision

Gervaise explores a future where computing becomes:

- continuous
- context‑aware
- local‑first
- distributed
- activity‑driven

The goal is not to build a better chatbot.

The goal is to redefine how humans interact with computers in the age of AI.

---

## One Sentence Vision

Gervaise is a **continuous intelligence system** where humans express intent once and the system orchestrates structured activities across their devices.
